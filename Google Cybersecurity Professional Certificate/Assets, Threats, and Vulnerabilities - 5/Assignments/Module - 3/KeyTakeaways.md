# Key Takeaways: Flaws in the System, Identify System Vulnerabilities, Cyber Attacker Mindset

---

# Flaws in the System

- System flaws are weaknesses in software, hardware, networks, or processes that attackers can exploit.
- Common causes of vulnerabilities include outdated software, weak passwords, poor configurations, and human error.
- The attack surface includes all possible points where attackers can gain unauthorized access.
- Security hardening helps reduce vulnerabilities by strengthening system configurations and limiting unnecessary access.
- Defense in depth uses multiple layers of security controls to reduce overall risk.
- Organizations must continuously monitor and update systems to minimize exposure to threats.

---

# Identify System Vulnerabilities

- Vulnerability assessments help organizations identify weaknesses before attackers exploit them.
- Vulnerability management is an ongoing process of discovering, prioritizing, and fixing security issues.
- Vulnerability scanners automatically detect known vulnerabilities across systems and networks.
- Different scans serve different purposes, including:
  - External scans
  - Internal scans
  - Authenticated scans
  - Unauthenticated scans
  - Limited scans
  - Comprehensive scans
- The CVE® list provides publicly known vulnerability information.
- CVSS scores help organizations measure vulnerability severity and prioritize remediation.
- Zero-day vulnerabilities are dangerous because fixes may not yet exist.
- Patch management is essential for reducing risks caused by outdated software.
- Penetration testing simulates real-world attacks to evaluate security defenses and identify weaknesses.

---

# Cyber Attacker Mindset

- Thinking like an attacker helps security professionals anticipate threats and strengthen defenses.
- Threat actors can include criminals, insiders, competitors, state-sponsored groups, and hacktivists.
- Attackers use various attack vectors such as phishing emails, social engineering, USB devices, and cloud services.
- Ethical hackers help organizations improve security by legally testing systems for vulnerabilities.
- Advanced Persistent Threats (APTs) involve attackers maintaining long-term unauthorized access to systems.
- Red team exercises simulate attacks, while blue teams focus on defense and incident response.
- Purple teams combine offensive and defensive strategies to improve overall security.
- OSINT (Open-Source Intelligence) helps organizations gather publicly available information about threats and vulnerabilities.
- Employee awareness and cybersecurity training are critical for reducing risks caused by human error and social engineering attacks.
