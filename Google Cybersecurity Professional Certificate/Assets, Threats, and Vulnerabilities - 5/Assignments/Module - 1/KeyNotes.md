# Key Notes: Assets and Risk in Cybersecurity

---

## Introduction to Assets

- An **asset** is anything that has value to an organization and must be protected.
- Assets are the foundation of cybersecurity because security efforts focus on protecting what is valuable.
- If an organization does not know its assets, it cannot properly secure them.
- Asset management involves identifying, tracking, and protecting all organizational resources.
- Assets are important because they support business operations, data storage, communication, and decision-making.

---

## Digital and Physical Assets

### Digital Assets
- Digital assets are information-based resources stored or processed electronically.
- Examples include:
  - Customer data
  - Databases
  - Emails and communication logs
  - Software applications
  - Cloud storage and systems
- These assets are highly valuable because they often contain sensitive or confidential information.
- They require strong protection such as encryption, access control, and monitoring.

### Physical Assets
- Physical assets are tangible items used in business operations.
- Examples include:
  - Computers and laptops
  - Servers and networking devices
  - Office equipment
  - Data centers and facilities
- Physical security measures help protect these assets (locks, access cards, surveillance).
- Physical assets are often entry points for digital attacks if not properly secured.

---

## Risk and Asset Security

- **Risk** refers to the possibility that something will negatively affect an asset’s confidentiality, integrity, or availability.
- Risk is typically calculated as:
  - **Risk = Likelihood × Impact**
- The goal of cybersecurity is to reduce risk, not eliminate it completely.

### Relationship Between Risk and Assets
- Assets must be identified before risks can be assessed.
- Each asset has different levels of importance and sensitivity.
- High-value assets require stronger security controls.

### Key Risk Factors
- **Threats:** Events or actions that can cause harm (intentional or accidental).
- **Vulnerabilities:** Weaknesses that can be exploited by threats.
- Risk increases when a threat can successfully exploit a vulnerability.

### Asset Security Practices
- Identifying and classifying assets based on importance
- Applying access controls to limit unauthorized access
- Monitoring systems for suspicious activity
- Regularly updating and patching systems
- Creating backup and recovery plans

---

## Summary

- Assets are everything valuable to an organization.
- They can be digital (data, systems) or physical (devices, infrastructure).
- Risk is the potential for harm to assets due to threats and vulnerabilities.
- Strong asset security depends on identifying assets, understanding risks, and applying proper protection controls.
