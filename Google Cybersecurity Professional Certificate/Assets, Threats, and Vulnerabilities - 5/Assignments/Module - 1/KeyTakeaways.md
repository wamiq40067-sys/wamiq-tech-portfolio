# Key Takeaways: Assets and Risk in Cybersecurity

---

## Introduction to Assets
- Assets are anything valuable to an organization that must be protected.
- Security efforts start by identifying what assets exist.
- Without knowing assets, organizations cannot properly manage risk.
- Asset awareness is the foundation of cybersecurity decision-making.
- Every asset contributes to business operations and needs some level of protection.

---

## Digital and Physical Assets
- Assets are broadly divided into digital and physical categories.
- Digital assets include data, software, databases, and cloud systems.
- Physical assets include hardware, devices, servers, and infrastructure.
- Digital assets are often more sensitive because they contain critical information.
- Physical assets require both physical security and cybersecurity controls.
- Both types of assets are interconnected and must be secured together.

---

## Risk and Asset Security
- Risk is the possibility that an asset will be harmed or exposed.
- Risk depends on two main factors: threats and vulnerabilities.
- A threat is anything that can cause harm, intentionally or unintentionally.
- A vulnerability is a weakness that can be exploited by a threat.
- Risk is commonly understood as: likelihood × impact.
- Strong asset security reduces risk by protecting against threats and fixing vulnerabilities.
- Protecting assets involves identification, classification, monitoring, and control measures.

---

## Overall Summary
- Assets are the core focus of cybersecurity protection.
- Both digital and physical assets require different but connected security approaches.
- Understanding risk helps prioritize which assets need stronger protection.
- Effective cybersecurity begins with knowing what to protect and why it matters.
