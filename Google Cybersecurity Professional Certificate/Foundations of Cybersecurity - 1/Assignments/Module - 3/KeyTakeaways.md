## Frameworks and Controls – Key Takeaways

- Cybersecurity frameworks provide structured guidelines that help organizations manage security risks in a consistent and organized way.

- They define best practices for identifying, protecting, detecting, responding to, and recovering from security threats.

- Security controls are safeguards used to reduce risk and protect systems, networks, and data from potential threats.

- Controls can be categorized into:
  - Preventive controls: reduce the chance of an attack occurring
  - Detective controls: identify and alert on security incidents
  - Corrective controls: help recover systems and fix damage after an incident

- Using frameworks and controls together helps organizations build a strong and layered security posture.


## Ethics in Cybersecurity – Key Takeaways

- Ethics ensures cybersecurity work is done in a responsible and trustworthy manner.
- Security professionals must protect user data and respect privacy at all times.
- Access to systems and sensitive information should never be misused.
- Ethical hackers disclose vulnerabilities responsibly to improve security.
- Legal compliance and professional conduct are essential parts of cybersecurity roles.
