## History and Evolution of Cybersecurity – Key Notes

- A computer virus is a type of malicious **software code** that disrupts normal system operations and can damage files or systems.

- The Morris worm played an important role in cybersecurity history by exposing early internet vulnerabilities, which led to increased focus on network security and incident response practices.

- The Equifax data breach highlighted major risks in data protection, especially the exposure of sensitive personal and financial information on a large scale.

- Social engineering techniques such as phishing involve tricking individuals into revealing confidential information by exploiting human behavior rather than technical system flaws.


## CISSP Security Domains – Study Notes

- Security domains refer to structured areas of knowledge such as:
  - Risk management
  - Security architecture and engineering

- Proper disposal of storage devices containing sensitive data falls under **asset security practices**, which ensure information is safely handled and destroyed when no longer needed.

- Reviewing and controlling access to sensitive data (like payroll information) relates to **identity and access management**, ensuring only authorized users can view or modify data.

- Investigating unknown or suspicious devices on a network and implementing preventive actions is part of **security operations and incident response**, focused on detecting and handling threats.
