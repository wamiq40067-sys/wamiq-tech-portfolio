## History and Evolution of Cybersecurity – Key Takeaways

- A computer virus is malicious **code** designed to disrupt system operations and cause damage.

- The Morris worm incident contributed to the creation and improvement of **computer emergency response teams (CERTs)** to handle future cyber incidents.

- The Equifax breach highlighted the severe financial and privacy impact of cyberattacks, including the exposure of millions of users’ personally identifiable information (PII).

- Social engineering attacks like phishing are based on human manipulation rather than system errors.  
  → Statement: False (it does not rely on computer errors)


  ## CISSP Security Domains – Key Takeaways

- Security domains are structured categories of cybersecurity knowledge, such as risk management, architecture, and engineering.

- Safely disposing of old storage devices containing sensitive data falls under **Asset Security**, which ensures proper handling and protection of information throughout its lifecycle.

- Reviewing user access permissions for sensitive systems like payroll is part of **Security Assessment and Testing**, which focuses on evaluating controls and access rights.

- Investigating unknown devices on a network and applying preventive controls falls under **Security Operations**, which deals with monitoring, detection, and response to threats.
