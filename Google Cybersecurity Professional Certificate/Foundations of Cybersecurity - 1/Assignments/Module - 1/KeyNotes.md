## Introduction to Cybersecurity – Key Notes

- The main goal of cybersecurity is to protect systems, networks, people, and data by ensuring they are not accessed or misused by unauthorized individuals.

- Entry-level security analysts typically support an organization by protecting systems, identifying potential vulnerabilities, and monitoring for suspicious or unusual activity.

- Reviewing logs, system records, and activity data to understand what is happening in a system is part of security monitoring and audit-related work.

- Cybersecurity teams add value to organizations by strengthening defense against threats, reducing risk, and helping meet legal and regulatory requirements.


## Core Skills for Cybersecurity Professionals – Key Notes

- Transferable skills are abilities that can be used across multiple careers and industries, not just cybersecurity. These include communication, analysis, and problem-solving.

- Technical skills for entry-level cybersecurity roles often include basic programming knowledge and the ability to analyze data and system behavior.

- Professionals who investigate cyber incidents and work with digital evidence are known as digital forensic investigators.

- Highly sensitive personal information (SPII) includes data such as financial details (like bank account numbers) and medical records, which must be strictly protected.
